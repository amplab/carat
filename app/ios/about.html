Carat: collaborative detection of energy bugs

Carat is a research project from the AMP Lab at UC Berkeley that helps detect and diagnose battery problems. After collecting data about your device for a week or more, it will give you personalized recommendations. The app uses a low-power feature of Location Services to be notified of significant changes in your location and to take samples. Carat does NOT use GPS or run in the background, period. If your battery is running down quickly, it isn't Carat's fault, but we hope it will help you discover the real culprit.

The Actions list tells you how to make your battery last longer and how much improvement to expect.

Bugs are apps that use a lot of energy on a small number of devices, including yours. Restarting a buggy app may improve battery life.

Hogs are apps that are correlated with higher energy use across many devices. Closing hogs may improve battery life.

Carat collects and reports generic usage statistics about your phone that allow it to do its job; this process involves absolutely no personally identifying information. Period.

Carat is under development, so please check back often to see new results. The more you use it, the better it will get. You can read more about the research on the Carat Project page and view the EULA here.
